## What Actually Happened

Without verified leaderboard data, we're going to resist the urge to speculate. What we *can* say:

**The team format remains a puzzle.** Talent alone doesn't win this event. The best individual players in the world routinely get bounced by teams with better chemistry and complementary games. Alternate shot, in particular, has a way of exposing partnerships that look good on paper but don't function on grass.

**TPC Louisiana is a course that rewards precision more than power.** The Pete Dye design features water on nearly half its holes and tight landing areas that punish wayward drives. In a team format, that means one partner's bad tee shot can cascade into a bogey (or worse) that the other can't recover from.

**The Zurich's place on the calendar matters.** Sitting between the majors hype machine — right after the Masters hangover and before the PGA Championship ramp-up — this event often gets undersold. But the team dynamic creates genuine tension that stroke-play weeks sometimes lack.

## Course & History

The Zurich Classic has been a fixture in New Orleans since 1938, making it one of the older events on Tour. It's been held annually since 1958 and moved to TPC Louisiana in Avondale — a suburb southwest of the city — where it's remained ever since. The switch to a team format in 2017 gave the tournament a distinct identity it had been lacking for years. Zurich Insurance Group sponsors the event, and it's organized by the Fore!Kids Foundation, which has funneled millions into youth programs across Louisiana. New Orleans itself adds a layer that most Tour stops can't match — this is one of the few weeks where the city outside the ropes is as much a draw as the golf inside them.
